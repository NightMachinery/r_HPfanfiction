So after +10 years reading HP fanfiction, I have come to a number of realizations regarding certain aspects of fanfiction. One of these is fanfiction lengths. When I get right down to it, the most innovative/unique/memorable fics I have read (in multiple fandoms) are ones that rarely exceed 100k words. Indeed I have found my favorites average between 40k and 80k words.

There is probably a lot of reasons for this, maybe regarding that longer fics are overwhelmingly by the book (hehe). They lack brevity (in prose, descriptiveness, focus) and indeed careful planning that makes shorter fics more memorable for me. This isn't to say longer fics cannot be good; there are multiple examples of such fics we can all name (and mostly agree on).

So I guess that what I am asking is what are your favorite shorter stories (i.e. coming in at under 80k or about the word length of Sorcerer's Stone for context). I don't really care for completeness (its often worse because of it), more that they are unique and entertaining.

Some of my favorites are linkffn(The Lesser Sadness; Skitterleap; The Unforgiving Minute; The Prisoner's Cipher; Concentric Wavelengths; The Crown of Metis; Harry the Hufflepuff; The One He Feared; Contractual Invalidation; Equal and Opposite; Heterochromic; The Dark Lord's Equal; Protection from Nargles)...

You get the idea.
