**** It's not that anything in the books disproves it. It's that the long hair was entirely the actor's idea. He talks about it in an interview, which is a great read. Here's an excerpt about the long hair:

#+begin_quote
  ‚ÄúI went to the set, and they had this idea of me wearing a pinstripe suit, short black-and-white hair,‚Äù Isaacs recalls. ‚ÄúI was slightly horrified. He was a racist, a eugenicist. There's no way he would cut his hair like a Muggle, or dress like a Muggle.‚Äù So Isaacs suggested instead that he wear a long white wig, and a particularly ostentatious wizard-like ensemble. ‚ÄúIn order to keep the hair straight, I had to tip my head back, so I was looking down my nose at everyone. There was 50 percent of the character. I asked for a walking stick, which [/Chamber of Secrets/ director] Chris Columbus first thought was because I had something wrong with my leg. I explained I wanted it as an affectation so I can pull my wand out [of the cane].
#+end_quote

I think Jason Isaacs deserves credit for these creative choices because they were great.

People also define canon differently. Some people say canon HP is just the books; others say it's the books + everything in the movies that doesn't directly contradict the books, but I believe this latter view detracts from the many artists who brought creativity to the series and go unrecognized for their contributions to the HP world.
